Cecilia Garraffo

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics working on modeling observations of X-ray emission from stellar coronae. My primary research thrust is employing the power and flexibility of the magnetohydrodynamic simulations to produce X-ray models that address forefront problems in astrophysics. Some examples are explaining the spin-down evolution of young, active stars; modeling exoplanetary environments in order to assess their habitability; modeling the orbital evolution of cataclysmic variables; and calibrating measurements of gyrochronology.
