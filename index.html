Cecilia Garraffo

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics working on modeling observations of X-ray emission from stellar coronae. My primary research thrust is employing the power and flexibility of the magnetohydrodynamic simulations to produce X-ray models that address forefront problems in astrophysics, for example, explaining the spin-down evolution of young, active stars; modeling exoplanetary environments in order to assess their habitability; modeling the orbital evolution of cataclysmic variables; and calibrating measurements of gyrochronology. My main interest is to address the challenging problem of spin-down evolution for young, active stars. Read more about my research...
